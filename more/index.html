Did you know...

  • Maya Civilization: The Maya were advanced astronomers, constructing their complex calendar system based on celestial movements. They believed the Milky Way was the pathway to the underworld and played a crucial role in predicting celestial events.
  • Ancient Greeks: To the Greeks, the galaxy was known as the "Milky Circle," created from the milk of Hera, queen of the gods. The constellations and stars were deeply integrated into their myths, heroes, and the very fabric of their culture.
  • Aboriginal Australians: One of the oldest cultures in the world, Aboriginal Australians have a rich tradition of astronomical knowledge. They saw the dark patches in the Milky Way as a celestial emu, and these star patterns are integral to their stories and cultural ceremonies.
